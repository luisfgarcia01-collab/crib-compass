Stay Safe

Avoid scams & protect yourself

Housing scams specifically target students. Know the red flags before you ever send a message — or a dollar.

🚩 Red flags to watch for

  • Price is unusually far below market rate
  • Landlord claims to be out of the country or unavailable in person
  • Asked to send deposit or first month before seeing the unit
  • Pressure to "claim it fast or lose it"
  • Only communication is via text or email — no phone/video calls
  • Can't verify the landlord owns the property
  • Requests payment via gift cards, Zelle, or wire transfer

✅ Safe steps before committing

  • Always tour the unit in person before paying anything
  • Look up property ownership records online (most counties have this free)
  • Search the address + "scam" or "reviews" before responding
  • Never wire money — use checks or payment with paper trail
  • Read the lease fully before signing anything
  • Ask for the landlord's full name and verify their identity
  • Get everything in writing — verbal promises mean nothing

Weekly Action Plan

Your 4-week roadmap to housing

Break the process into manageable chunks. Tackle one week at a time and you'll be signing a lease before you know it.

Week 1
Budget + Research
  • Set your max budget per month
  • Decide on housing type and roommates
  • Research neighborhoods around campus
  • Make a list of must-haves vs. nice-to-haves
Week 2
Reach Out + Shortlist
  • Use message templates to contact landlords
  • Shortlist 3–5 viable options
  • Screen for red flags / scam indicators
  • Confirm availability and pricing details
Week 3
Tours + Document Prep
  • Schedule and complete in-person tours
  • Take photos and notes at each visit
  • Gather application documents
  • Confirm co-signer if needed
Week 4
Apply + Decide
  • Submit applications to top choices
  • Review lease carefully before signing
  • Clarify any unclear terms in writing
  • Finalize and sign — you've got a home!
